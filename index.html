Phases

1. Online Elimination Round


February 12 - February 23, 2025

The Online Elimination Round of CodeCraze 2.0 assesses teams' strategic thinking, creativity, and problem-solving skills. Teams can choose from provided problem statements or propose their own, subject to organizer approval within two days. A PPT presentation of their solution is required, with no prototype needed. The top 40 teams will be shortlisted based on innovation, feasibility, presentation, and impact to advance to the Offline Hackathon.


  • Teams can choose from provided problem statements or submit their own, provided it is moderate and aligns with the societal and moral impact theme.
  • Self-proposed problem statements will go through a screening process.
  • Teams will be notified via email if rejected within 2 days of registration. If no email is received, the statement is considered accepted.
  • Teams must submit an idea presentation (PPT). No working prototype is required.
  • Results Announcement: February 25, 2025.

2. Offline Hackathon


February 27-28, 2025

The Offline Hackathon is the final phase of CodeCraze 2.0, where the top 40 teams from Phase 1 compete in a 24-hour on-campus challenge. Teams must build a working prototype from scratch, focusing on originality, execution, and impact. Open-source innovation is encouraged, but prebuilt solutions are prohibited. AI tools may be used to enhance development.


  • The top 40 teams from Phase 1 will compete.
  • Teams must build a prototype from scratch at the venue.
  • Use of prebuilt code will result in disqualification.
  • AI tools are allowed, but prebuilt solutions are prohibited.
  • Prototype Requirement: 40-50% working prototype.
